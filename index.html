Music Background

Music has been an integral part of my life from an early age, shaping both my personal identity and creative expression. Growing up in a Hispanic household, music was more than just background noise—it was a thread that connected family, tradition, and culture. From lively gatherings to quiet moments of reflection, I learned to appreciate the depth, diversity, and emotional power of music in everyday life.

During high school, I had the honor of performing with a world-class ensemble, an experience that not only pushed my technical and collaborative skills to new heights but also showed me the transformative power of music when shared with others. These performances taught me discipline, resilience, and the joy of creating something larger than myself.

Beyond performance, I explore music through composition, using it as a personal outlet to express ideas and emotions that words alone cannot convey. Composing allows me to combine technique, creativity, and cultural inspiration into works that are uniquely my own. This blend of family, heritage, performance, and creation continues to inspire my musical journey and drives my passion for exploring new ways to communicate through sound.

Arrangements

Bring Me to Life - Percussion Arrangement

A lively and engaging arrangement designed to be enjoyable for performers. Each instrument explores distinct styles while maintaining momentum, with marimba and vibraphone driving the rhythm in this drumless piece. The piece highlights the energy and excitement that comes from performing together.

Merry Go Round of Life - Percussion Arrangement

A nostalgic reinterpretation of a beloved piece, arranged with playful percussion textures. Drawing inspiration from the Blue Devils 2024 arrangement, it captures both the charm and excitement of the original while providing a fun and creative experience for performers.

Clair de Lune - Small Ensemble Arrangement

A delicate, faithful adaptation for flute, violin, and piano that preserves the melancholic beauty of the original. The arrangement evokes a liminal space, a bittersweet sense of what once was, highlighted through the interplay of melody and texture that reflects the reflective and introspective nature of the piece.
